# How to add your email signature to Gmail

*These steps take about 2 minutes. Do this on a computer, not your phone.*

Your signature link:

> `https://agent-marketing-desk.github.io/email-signature/clients/YOUR-NAME/signature.html`

*(Your exact link is in the email we sent you.)*

## Steps

1. **Open your signature link** in your web browser (Chrome, Safari, or Edge)
2. **Select everything** on the page: press **Cmd+A** (Mac) or **Ctrl+A** (Windows)
3. **Copy it**: press **Cmd+C** (Mac) or **Ctrl+C** (Windows)
4. Open **Gmail**, click the **gear icon** (top right), then click **"See all settings"**
5. Stay on the **General** tab and scroll down to **Signature**
6. Click **"+ Create new"**, give it a name (e.g. "My Signature"), and click **Create**
7. Click inside the signature box and **paste**: **Cmd+V** (Mac) or **Ctrl+V** (Windows) — you should see your signature appear with your photo and logo
8. Just below the box, under **Signature defaults**, set:
   - **For new emails use:** your new signature
   - **On reply/forward use:** your new signature
9. Scroll to the very bottom of the page and click **Save Changes**
10. **Test it:** compose a new email to yourself — your signature should appear automatically

## Good to know

- **Your photo and logo load from the web**, so they'll show up for everyone you email — nothing extra to attach or upload.
- **Gmail phone app:** the Gmail app has its own separate "Mobile signature" setting. Leave that **turned off** — emails sent from your phone will then use this same signature automatically.
- **The animation** (the handwritten "Thank you") plays in most email apps. Outlook on Windows shows it as a still image — that's normal.
- If the signature ever needs an update (new phone number, new brokerage), contact us — you'll just repeat these steps once with the refreshed version.
